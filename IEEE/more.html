Reduce, Reuse, Recycle

Learn how reducing, reusing, and recycling can help you, your community, and the environment by saving money, energy, and natural resources. Recycling programs are managed at the state and local level—find information on recycling in your community. On the national level, EPA is working to build an economy that keeps materials, products, and services in circulation for as long as possible, what's known as a “circular economy.”

  • Reduce Plastic Usage:

    Use Reusable Bags:

    Carry reusable shopping bags made of cloth, jute, or other sustainable materials. Avoid single-use plastic bags.

    Refuse Plastic Straws:

    Say no to plastic straws when you order drinks at restaurants. You can carry reusable metal or bamboo straws if needed.

    Switch to Reusable Bottles:

    Use stainless steel or glass water bottles instead of disposable plastic bottles. It reduces plastic waste and is often safer for your health.

    Choose Reusable Containers:

    Opt for reusable food containers made of glass, stainless steel, or silicone instead of single-use plastic ones

    • Recycling:

    Resource Conservation:

    Recycling helps conserve valuable natural resources such as metals, paper, and plastics by reusing them in the production of new products.

    Energy Savings:

    Recycling often requires less energy than producing items from raw materials. For example, recycling aluminum saves up to 95% of the energy compared to creating aluminum from bauxite ore.

    Reduction in Landfill Waste:

    Recycling diverts materials from landfills, reducing the burden on waste disposal sites and helping to extend their lifespan.

    Conservation of Biodiversity:

    Reducing the need for raw materials extraction through recycling can help protect natural habitats and biodiversity

    Environmental Impact:

    Climate Change

    : Human activities, especially the burning of fossil fuels for energy and transportation, are a significant driver of climate change. This leads to rising global temperatures, sea-level rise, and extreme weather events.

    Air Pollution

    : Emissions from vehicles, factories, and power plants release pollutants into the air, leading to smog, respiratory diseases, and other health issues. It also harms ecosystems and wildlife.

    Loss of Biodiversity

    : Habitat destruction, pollution, and overexploitation of resources have led to a rapid decline in biodiversity.
